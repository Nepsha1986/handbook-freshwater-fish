## Overview

The Featherfin Squeaker (_Synodontis eupterus_) is one of the most recognizable members of the Mochokidae family, native
to the rivers and lakes of Central and West Africa, primarily in the Niger and Chad river basins. The hallmark of this
catfish is its tall dorsal fin with greatly elongated rays, resembling a feather or veil, which gives the species its
common name.

The body of _S. eupterus_ is robust and slightly laterally compressed, colored grayish-brown with numerous dark spots.
With age, the pattern becomes less contrasting. A characteristic feature shared by all synodontis species is three pairs
of sensitive barbels around the mouth, which help the fish navigate murky waters and search for food along the bottom.
In aquariums, these catfish grow to 15–20 cm and can live 8–12 years with proper care.

This species is predominantly nocturnal: it hides among decorations and driftwood during the day and begins actively
exploring the aquarium at dusk in search of food. Despite its impressive size, _Synodontis eupterus_ has a calm
disposition and coexists well with similarly sized tankmates, making it an excellent candidate for a spacious community
aquarium.

## Tank Requirements

Keeping the Featherfin Squeaker comfortably requires an aquarium of **at least 150 liters**. Given the fish's nocturnal
activity, it is important to provide sufficient open swimming space along the bottom.

Optimal water parameters:

- **Temperature:** 23–28 °C
- **pH:** 6.5–7.5
- **Hardness (GH):** 4–15

Fine sand or smooth-edged gravel works best as substrate — this prevents damage to the fish's sensitive barbels. Be sure
to provide plenty of hiding spots: driftwood, ceramic tubes, rock formations, and caves. Live plants with strong root
systems (Anubias, Cryptocoryne, ferns) are appropriate and will create shaded areas that the catfish will appreciate
during daylight hours.

Filtration should be efficient, with moderate current to simulate natural riverine conditions. Aeration is essential —
_S. eupterus_ prefers well-oxygenated water. Subdued lighting is recommended; under bright light, the fish will
constantly hide and experience stress.

## Feeding and Diet

_Synodontis eupterus_ is an omnivorous species that feeds on insects, larvae, crustaceans, plant matter, and detritus in
the wild. In the aquarium, the foundation of its diet should be high-quality sinking pellets or catfish wafers,
supplemented with:

- **Live and frozen foods:** bloodworms, brine shrimp, daphnia, tubifex
- **Vegetable supplements:** blanched spinach leaves, zucchini or cucumber slices, spirulina tablets

Feeding is best done in the evening, after the main lighting is turned off or under moonlight mode — this aligns with
the fish's natural rhythms and ensures the food reaches the catfish rather than more active daytime tankmates.
Once-daily feeding is sufficient; a fasting day once a week is beneficial. Monitor for uneaten food accumulating on the
bottom — removing it promptly helps maintain water quality.

## Care and Maintenance

The Featherfin Squeaker is a hardy species that does not demand excessive care, though stability of water parameters
remains the key factor for its health. Weekly water changes of 20–25% of the total volume are essential. Regularly test
ammonia, nitrite, and nitrate levels using a water testing kit.

Pay special attention to substrate cleanliness — vacuum the bottom at least once a week, especially in feeding zones.
Clean the filter media in aquarium water every two to three weeks to prevent excessive sludge buildup.

The most common health issues are bacterial infections and ich (white spot disease), which typically arise from
deteriorating water quality or sudden temperature fluctuations. Like most catfish, _S. eupterus_ is sensitive to
copper-based medications, so when treating illness, choose products that are safe for scaleless fish. Prevention through
maintaining cleanliness and stable conditions is always the best approach.

## Compatibility

_Synodontis eupterus_ is a relatively peaceful species that coexists well with most similarly sized fish. Ideal
tankmates are medium to large species with a calm temperament. Good companions include:

- Large characins such as the [Congo tetra](/en/phenacogrammus-interruptus)
- Peaceful African cichlids
- Other synodontis species such as the [Angel Squeaker](/en/synodontis-angelicus) or
  [Cuckoo Catfish](/en/synodontis-multipunctatus)
- Large barbs and rainbowfish

Avoid housing with very small fish (neon tetras, micro rasboras) — they may be accidentally swallowed at night.
Aggressive territorial species that would harass the slow-moving catfish are also unsuitable. When keeping multiple
synodontis in the same aquarium, provide each individual with its own hiding spot to prevent territorial disputes.

## Breeding

Breeding _Synodontis eupterus_ in home aquariums is extremely challenging, and successful spawning events in captivity
are rare. In commercial breeding, hormonal injections are used to stimulate spawning.

For a breeding attempt, a separate spawning tank of at least 100 liters is needed with soft, slightly acidic water (pH
around 6.5, GH below 8). Raise the temperature to 26–28 °C and perform frequent cool water changes to simulate the onset
of the rainy season — the natural spawning trigger. Fine-leaved plants or synthetic spawning mops serve as substrate for
egg deposition.

The female scatters eggs, which the male fertilizes. After spawning, the parents must be removed, as they will eat their
own eggs. Incubation takes 3–4 days, after which the larvae feed on their yolk sacs. First food for the fry includes
infusoria and freshly hatched brine shrimp nauplii, gradually transitioning to crushed dry foods. Fry grow slowly and
require especially clean water during the first weeks of life.
