## Overview

The Ram Cichlid (_Mikrogeophagus ramirezi_) is one of the most popular and recognizable dwarf cichlids in the aquarium
hobby. This small fish, reaching 6-8 cm in length, inhabits the Orinoco and Meta river basins in Venezuela and Colombia.
Despite being commonly referred to as "Apistogramma Ramirezi," this species does not belong to the genus Apistogramma
but rather to the genus _Mikrogeophagus_.

The Ram Cichlid boasts incredibly vibrant coloration, with its body shimmering in blue, yellow, and orange hues, and a
distinctive black spot on its flanks. Males are larger than females and have elongated first rays of the dorsal fin.
Numerous selectively bred morphs are available in the aquarium trade: gold, electric blue, balloon, and long-fin
varieties, each with its own unique characteristics.

In terms of temperament, the Ram Cichlid is one of the most peaceful cichlids, making it an excellent choice for
community tanks with other calm species. With proper care, they can live for 3-5 years.

## Tank Requirements

Ram Cichlids require an aquarium of at least 100 liters. The optimal water temperature is 24-28 °C, pH should be
maintained at 6.0-7.5, and general hardness at 6-12 dGH. Rams are sensitive to water quality, so the aquarium must be
fully cycled and biologically mature before introducing these fish.

Fine substrate such as sand or small gravel works best, as these fish enjoy sifting through it in search of food. The
tank should be densely planted with live vegetation, creating shaded areas and hiding spots. Driftwood and rocks help
establish territorial boundaries, which is especially important when keeping multiple pairs. Moderate or subdued
lighting is preferred.

Efficient filtration is essential, but water flow should not be too strong. Weekly water changes of 20-25% help maintain
stable parameters.

## Feeding and Diet

Ram Cichlids are omnivorous with a preference for animal-based foods. In the wild, they feed on small invertebrates,
insect larvae, and detritus. In the aquarium, their diet should be based on high-quality dry food in the form of small
pellets, supplemented with live and frozen foods such as brine shrimp, bloodworms, daphnia, and tubifex.

Dietary variety is the key to maintaining health and vibrant coloration. Feed 2-3 times daily in small portions that the
fish can consume within 2-3 minutes. Overfeeding must be avoided, as leftover food degrades water quality, to which Rams
are particularly sensitive.

## Care and Maintenance

The Ram Cichlid requires attentive and consistent care. Daily tasks include checking water temperature and equipment
operation, as well as observing fish behavior. Any changes in coloration or activity may signal problems.

Perform weekly water changes of 20-25%, clean algae from the glass, and test water parameters regularly. Parameter
stability is critically important — sudden fluctuations in temperature or pH can lead to stress and disease. Rams are
susceptible to ich (white spot disease) and bacterial infections when conditions deteriorate.

Monitor the filter condition and rinse filter media in aquarium water as needed. Keep the substrate clean by removing
leftover food and organic waste using a gravel vacuum.

## Compatibility

The Ram Cichlid is a peaceful fish that works well in community tanks with calm tank mates. Excellent companions include
small characins such as [neon tetras](/en/paracheirodon-innesi), [cardinal tetras](/en/paracheirodon-axelrodi), and
[ember tetras](/en/hyphessobrycon-amandae). Peaceful bottom-dwellers like [panda corydoras](/en/corydoras-panda) or
[Sterbai corydoras](/en/corydoras-sterbai) also make great neighbors.

Among related species, the [Bolivian Ram](/en/mikrogeophagus-altispinosus) is highly compatible, as are peaceful
Apistogramma species such as [Borelli's dwarf cichlid](/en/apistogramma-borellii). However, when keeping multiple pairs
of cichlids, sufficient territories and hiding spots must be provided.

Avoid housing Ram Cichlids with large or aggressive fish that may intimidate them. Active fish that create strong
competition for food are also undesirable tank mates.

## Breeding

Breeding _Mikrogeophagus ramirezi_ in the aquarium is an achievable goal even for beginner aquarists. Rams are open
substrate spawners that lay their eggs on flat surfaces such as rocks, plant leaves, or even the walls of clay pots.

To stimulate spawning, raise the water temperature to 27-28 °C and provide soft, slightly acidic water. The male
attracts the female with vibrant displays and prepares the spawning site. The female lays 150-300 eggs, which both
parents diligently guard and fan with their fins to ensure a fresh supply of oxygenated water.

The larvae hatch within 2-3 days, and after another 4-5 days, the fry become free-swimming. Both parents continue caring
for their offspring, which is a hallmark of this species — biparental care. Infusoria and brine shrimp nauplii serve as
first foods for the fry. When breeding selectively bred morphs, be aware that some varieties, particularly balloon
types, may exhibit weakened parental instincts.
