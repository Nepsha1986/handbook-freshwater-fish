## Overview

_Notropis chrosomus_, commonly known as the Rainbow Shiner, is one of the most beautiful freshwater fish native to North
America. This species belongs to the family Cyprinidae and inhabits clear streams and tributaries of the Coosa River
system in the southeastern United States — Alabama, Georgia, and Tennessee. Adults reach a size of 5-8 cm and live 3-5
years with proper care.

The most remarkable feature of _Notropis chrosomus_ is the spectacular nuptial coloration of males. During the breeding
season (spring), males develop iridescent pink, violet, and blue hues across their head and body, making them among the
most striking freshwater fish in the world. Outside the breeding season, both sexes display a plain silver coloration.
This is a cold-water species with a temperature range of 15-22°C, which is an essential consideration when planning an
aquarium setup.

## Tank Requirements

_Notropis chrosomus_ is a stream-dwelling fish accustomed to clean waterways with moderate to strong current. The
minimum tank volume is 60 liters, though a tank of 100 liters or more is preferable for a school of 8-10 individuals.
Water temperature must be maintained within 15-22°C — this is a critically important parameter, as the species cannot
tolerate tropical temperatures. The optimal pH is 6.5-8.0, with general hardness (GH) of 5-12.

The aquarium should be equipped with a powerful filter that generates noticeable current, along with strong aeration,
since these fish naturally inhabit fast-flowing, oxygen-rich streams. Fine gravel or coarse sand works well as a
substrate. Add rocks of various sizes and driftwood to create a natural landscape, while leaving ample open swimming
space. Live plants that tolerate cooler water will complement the biotope nicely.

## Feeding and Diet

In the wild, _Notropis chrosomus_ feeds on small invertebrates, insect larvae, algae, and plant matter. In the aquarium,
these fish readily accept high-quality flakes and small pellets as a staple diet.

To maintain health and encourage vivid nuptial coloration, regularly supplement their diet with live or frozen foods
such as daphnia, brine shrimp, bloodworms, and cyclops. A varied diet is especially important during the pre-spawning
period. Feed twice daily in small portions, ensuring all food is consumed within a few minutes.

## Care and Maintenance

_Notropis chrosomus_ is a relatively undemanding species (care level 2 out of 5), but it does require one key condition
— cool, clean water. Weekly water changes of 25-30% are essential. Filtration should be powerful enough to provide both
mechanical cleaning and water movement. Aeration is critically important, as these fish are sensitive to low oxygen
levels.

Avoid sudden fluctuations in temperature and water chemistry. During summer, if room temperature rises above 22°C, you
may need to use an aquarium chiller or fans to cool the water. This is a schooling species — keep at least 8 individuals
together so the fish feel secure and display natural behavior.

## Compatibility

The Rainbow Shiner is a peaceful schooling fish (aggression level 2 out of 5) that is well-suited for a community
aquarium with other cold-water species. It is essential to keep them in groups of 8 or more, where males will display
their finest colors while competing for female attention.

Excellent tank mates include [White Cloud Mountain Minnows](/en/tanichthys-albonubes), small danios, and cold-tolerant
corydoras such as [Panda Corydoras](/en/corydoras-panda) and [Pygmy Corydoras](/en/corydoras-pygmaeus). Avoid housing
them with large, aggressive, or strictly tropical fish that require water warmer than 24°C. The primary principle for
selecting tank mates is temperature compatibility.

## Breeding

Breeding _Notropis chrosomus_ in the aquarium is achievable but requires some effort (difficulty 3 out of 5). The key
stimulus for spawning is simulating seasonal changes. During winter, keep the fish at 15-17°C, then gradually raise the
temperature to 20-22°C in spring, which triggers breeding behavior.

It is during this period that males develop their famous nuptial coloration — iridescent pink, violet, and blue tones
cover the head and body, creating a truly breathtaking display. For spawning, prepare an area with fine gravel and flat
rocks where females can scatter their eggs. After spawning, remove the adults to prevent them from eating the eggs. The
eggs develop over 3-5 days, after which the fry can be fed infusoria and microworms, gradually transitioning to baby
brine shrimp.
