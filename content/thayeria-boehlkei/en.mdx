## Overview

_Thayeria boehlkei_ is a small characin native to the Amazon basin, specifically the Araguaia and Tocantins river
systems in Brazil. It earned the common name "Penguin Tetra" due to its characteristic swimming posture — the body is
held at an angle with the head tilted upward, reminiscent of a penguin's stance. A bold black stripe running from the
mid-body down to the lower lobe of the caudal fin makes this species instantly recognizable among other tetras.

Adults reach 5–6 cm in length. The body is elongated and slightly compressed laterally, with a silvery base color and an
olive tint along the back. In a school, _Thayeria boehlkei_ create a mesmerizing display as they sway in unison at their
characteristic angle. With proper care, they live for 5–6 years. Thanks to their hardiness and peaceful temperament,
this species is an excellent choice for beginner aquarists.

## Tank Requirements

A school of 8–10 _Thayeria boehlkei_ requires an aquarium of at least 70 liters. These fish are active swimmers, so it
is important to leave ample open space in the middle and upper water column.

Optimal water parameters:

- Temperature: 22–28 °C
- pH: 6.0–7.5
- General hardness (GH): 5–15 dGH

Fine dark sand or gravel works well as a substrate — it brings out the fishes' colors beautifully. Plant the perimeter
and background with vegetation, leaving the central area open for swimming. Vallisneria, Echinodorus, and Cryptocoryne
species are all suitable choices. Floating plants will help create subdued lighting that closely mimics their natural
habitat.

Filtration should be efficient but without a strong current — a moderate flow is sufficient. A tight-fitting lid or
cover glass is essential: Penguin Tetras are prone to jumping, especially during the acclimatization period.

## Feeding and Diet

_Thayeria boehlkei_ are omnivorous fish with hearty appetites. In the wild, they feed on small insects, larvae, and
plant particles. In the aquarium, they readily accept most types of food.

A staple diet of quality flakes or small granules for tropical fish works well. To maintain vibrant coloration and good
health, regularly supplement with live or frozen foods: daphnia, brine shrimp, bloodworms, and cyclops. Vegetable
supplements such as spirulina or blanched spinach leaves are also beneficial.

Feed twice daily in small portions that the fish consume within 2–3 minutes. Overfeeding is one of the most common
mistakes — it leads to obesity and deteriorating water quality. A weekly fasting day is a good practice.

## Care and Maintenance

The Penguin Tetra is an undemanding fish, but stability of water conditions is essential. Weekly water changes of 20–25%
will help maintain cleanliness and a stable chemical balance.

Test water parameters regularly: ammonia and nitrite should always read zero, and nitrate levels should remain below
20–30 mg/l. Vacuum the substrate during water changes and remove any dead plant leaves.

Keep _Thayeria boehlkei_ in groups of at least 6–8 individuals — in solitude or small groups, these fish become timid
and lose their color. In a properly sized school, they display natural behavior and vivid coloration. Moderate lighting
is preferred, as overly bright light causes stress.

Among common diseases, ich (white spot disease) is the most notable concern; tetras are susceptible to it during sudden
temperature fluctuations and periods of stress. Quarantining new fish before adding them to the main tank is a mandatory
precaution.

## Compatibility

_Thayeria boehlkei_ is one of the most peaceful tetras, making it an excellent candidate for a community aquarium. It
coexists beautifully with other calm species of similar size.

Suitable tank mates:

- Small tetras: [neon tetra](/en/paracheirodon-innesi), [cardinal tetra](/en/paracheirodon-axelrodi),
  [glowlight tetra](/en/hemigrammus-erythrozonus), [flame tetra](/en/hyphessobrycon-flammeus)
- Rasboras: [harlequin rasbora](/en/trigonostigma-heteromorpha), [Hengel's rasbora](/en/trigonostigma-hengeli)
- Danios: [zebra danio](/en/danio-rerio), [celestial pearl danio](/en/danio-margaritatus)
- Catfish: [panda corydoras](/en/corydoras-panda), [Sterbai corydoras](/en/corydoras-sterbai),
  [pygmy corydoras](/en/corydoras-pygmaeus)

Avoid housing _Thayeria boehlkei_ with large or aggressive cichlids, as well as fish with long, flowing fins that the
tetras may occasionally nip. Larger predatory species will view these small tetras as food.

## Breeding

Breeding _Thayeria boehlkei_ at home requires some preparation but is entirely achievable. Set up a separate breeding
tank of 20–30 liters with soft, slightly acidic water (pH 6.0–6.5, GH 4–8) and a temperature of 25–27 °C.

Place fine-leaved plants (Java moss, Cabomba) or a synthetic spawning mop on the bottom. A mesh screen on the tank floor
will help protect the eggs from being eaten by the parents. Lighting should be minimal — these fish spawn in dim
conditions.

Select a well-fed female with a rounded belly and the most vibrantly colored male. Feed them generously with live foods
for a week before spawning. The female scatters 300–500 small eggs among the plants. Remove the adults immediately after
spawning.

The fry hatch within 24–36 hours and become free-swimming on days 3–4. Initial food should be infusoria or liquid fry
food. After about a week, they can be transitioned to baby brine shrimp. The fry grow relatively quickly, and by 2–3
months of age they develop the characteristic stripe and angled swimming posture of adults.
