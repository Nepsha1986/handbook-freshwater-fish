## Overview

_Thorichthys aureus_, commonly known as the gold firemouth cichlid, is one of the most attractive members of the
_Thorichthys_ genus. This cichlid inhabits slow-moving rivers and lakes of Guatemala, Belize, and Honduras, where it
prefers areas with soft sandy bottoms and abundant vegetation. In the wild, the fish occupies shaded shallows with dense
shoreline plant cover.

Adults reach a size of 12-15 cm and can live 10-12 years with proper care. The body features a striking metallic blue
sheen with golden accents along the flanks and fins. Males are typically larger than females, display more vivid
coloration, and have elongated dorsal and anal fins. During spawning, color intensity increases significantly in both
sexes.

Unlike its close relative the [firemouth cichlid](/en/thorichthys-meeki), _T. aureus_ has a less pronounced red throat
patch and a more saturated blue body color. This fish is ideal for aquarists who already have experience keeping
cichlids and are looking for a colorful yet not overly aggressive species.

## Tank Requirements

For comfortable keeping of _Thorichthys aureus_, an aquarium of at least 150 liters is required for a pair. Optimal
water parameters are: temperature 24-28°C, pH 7.0-8.0, hardness GH 8-15. The fish prefers moderate water flow and
well-oxygenated water.

Fine, light-colored sand works best as a substrate -- gold firemouths enjoy sifting through it in search of food, and
coarse gravel can damage their gill covers. The aquarium should be decorated with plenty of hiding spots using rocks,
driftwood, and ceramic caves. This allows the fish to establish territories and reduces stress levels.

Live plants are welcome, but choose hardy species (Anubias, Cryptocoryne) as delicate plants may be uprooted. Filtration
should be robust -- an external canister filter rated at 5-8 times the tank volume per hour is an optimal choice.
Moderate or subdued lighting is preferable, as it highlights the metallic shimmer of the scales.

## Feeding and Diet

In its natural habitat, _Thorichthys aureus_ feeds on small invertebrates, insect larvae, and detritus by sifting
through the sandy bottom. In the aquarium, the fish is undemanding and readily accepts a variety of foods.

The staple diet should consist of high-quality pellets or flakes designed for medium-sized cichlids. As supplements,
regularly offer frozen bloodworms, brine shrimp, daphnia, or glassworms. Live foods are also eagerly consumed and
encourage natural foraging behavior.

Vegetable supplementation is essential for a balanced diet. Blanched spinach, zucchini slices, or spirulina-based foods
will provide the necessary fiber. Feed 2-3 times daily in small portions that are consumed within 2-3 minutes.
Overfeeding is one of the most common mistakes, leading to deteriorating water quality and digestive issues.

## Care and Maintenance

Consistent maintenance is the key to keeping _Thorichthys aureus_ healthy. Weekly water changes of 25-30% are mandatory
to maintain stable parameters. Use water of the same temperature during changes to avoid stress. It is advisable to
monitor water parameters with test kits at least once a week, especially during the first months after setting up the
aquarium.

Vacuum the substrate regularly, paying special attention to areas around hiding spots where uneaten food accumulates.
Clean the glass of algae buildup and rinse filter sponges in aquarium water every 2-3 weeks.

Watch for behavioral changes: loss of appetite, faded coloration, or clamped fins may indicate illness or stress. _T.
aureus_ can be susceptible to common cichlid diseases such as ich and hole-in-the-head disease, particularly when water
quality is poor. Maintaining stable parameters and providing a varied diet is the best prevention.

## Compatibility

_Thorichthys aureus_ has a moderate temperament, making it one of the more peaceful Central American cichlids. However,
during breeding the fish actively defends its territory, and tank mates should be able to handle this.

Ideal companions include other Central American cichlids of comparable size and temperament:
[firemouth cichlid](/en/thorichthys-meeki), [Nicaraguan cichlid](/en/cichlasoma-nicaraguense), or
[convict cichlid](/en/amatitlania-nigrofasciata). They can also be kept with large characins and catfish (Corydoras,
Ancistrus).

Avoid housing with large aggressive species such as the [jaguar cichlid](/en/parachromis-managuensis) or
[Salvini cichlid](/en/trichromis-salvini), which may dominate the gold firemouth. Small fish (neon tetras, guppies) are
also unsuitable, as they may be seen as prey. _T. aureus_ does best as a pair or in a small group of 4-6 individuals in
a sufficiently spacious aquarium.

## Breeding

Breeding _Thorichthys aureus_ is not particularly challenging and is well within reach of an intermediate-level
aquarist. The fish form stable monogamous pairs that may bond for life.

To stimulate spawning, raise the water temperature by 1-2°C and increase the proportion of live food in the diet. The
pair selects a smooth surface -- a flat rock, cave wall, or even the aquarium glass -- and thoroughly cleans it before
spawning. The female deposits 200-400 eggs, which both parents zealously guard.

Incubation lasts 2-3 days, after which the parents move the larvae to a pre-dug pit in the substrate. Within 5-7 days
the fry become free-swimming. At this stage, they can be fed newly hatched brine shrimp and microworms. The parents
continue to guard their offspring for another 3-4 weeks, and observing their devoted parental behavior is one of the
most fascinating spectacles in the hobby. As the fry grow, transition them to crushed dry food and small bloodworms.
