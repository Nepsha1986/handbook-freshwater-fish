## Overview

**Aulonocara jacobfreibergi**, commonly known as Jacob's Cichlid, is a stunning species from Lake Tanganyika in East
Africa. This fish is noted for its vibrant coloration and intriguing behavior. Adults can reach up to 15 cm in length.
In the wild, they inhabit rocky areas of the lake, finding shelter and feeding on various small organisms.

## Feeding and Diet

In the wild, **Aulonocara jacobfreibergi** feeds on small aquatic organisms. In the aquarium, offer high-quality flakes,
pellets, and live or frozen foods like daphnia, bloodworms, or brine shrimp. Feed them 2-3 times daily to maintain their
health and vibrant coloration.

## Care and Maintenance

To keep **Aulonocara jacobfreibergi** healthy, perform regular water changes of 10-20% weekly. Maintain a clean tank by
cleaning the filter and monitoring water parameters. Watch for any signs of illness or stress to ensure their
well-being.

## Compatibility

**Aulonocara jacobfreibergi** can be aggressive towards other fish, especially within their own species. It is best to
house them with other peaceful cichlids or fish that can tolerate their behavior. Avoid keeping them with smaller or
more passive species.

## Breeding

To successfully breed **Aulonocara jacobfreibergi**, create a suitable breeding environment with hiding spots. The fish
will exhibit interesting courtship behaviors. The female lays eggs, which she incubates in her mouth. After hatching,
separate the fry from the adults to ensure their survival.
