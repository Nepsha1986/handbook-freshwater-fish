## Overview

_Tetraodon biocellatus_ is one of the most popular members of the family Tetraodontidae in the aquarium hobby. Its
common name comes from the distinctive figure-eight pattern on its back. This small but incredibly charismatic fish
originates from the brackish waters of Southeast Asia — mangrove estuaries and coastal rivers of Thailand, Malaysia,
Indonesia, and Borneo.

Adults reach 6–8 cm in length and can live 5 to 10 years with proper care. Despite their modest size, _Tetraodon
biocellatus_ has a vivid personality: it recognizes its owner, begs for food, and curiously watches everything happening
around the tank. However, behind this charm hides a predator with powerful dental plates and a challenging temperament,
making the Figure Eight Puffer a fish that requires some experience to keep. Unlike the miniature
[Dwarf Puffer](/en/carinotetraodon-travancoricus), this species prefers brackish water, which makes its husbandry more
specialized.

## Tank Requirements

A minimum tank volume of 100 liters is required for keeping _Tetraodon biocellatus_. Optimal water parameters are:
temperature 24–28 °C, pH 7.0–8.0, and general hardness (GH) 8–12. The most critical factor is the addition of marine
salt. The Figure Eight Puffer is a brackish-water species, and a salinity level of 3–5 grams of marine salt per liter is
recommended for comfortable keeping. Without brackish conditions, the fish may gradually lose immunity and become prone
to disease.

The tank should be equipped with a powerful filter providing good mechanical and biological filtration — puffers produce
a significant amount of waste. Sandy substrate or fine gravel works best. Decorations should include driftwood, rocks,
and caves that create plenty of hiding spots. Live plants can be used, but be prepared for the puffer to nibble on them
— hardy species such as Anubias and Java moss are the best choices.

## Feeding and Diet

Feeding is one of the most critical aspects of keeping _Tetraodon biocellatus_. This fish is an obligate carnivore, and
the core of its diet should consist of live and frozen animal-based foods. It is vitally important to regularly offer
hard-shelled prey: snails (ramshorn, bladder, Malaysian trumpet snails), small shrimp, mussels, and clams. The puffer's
dental plates grow continuously throughout its life, and without hard food, they can become overgrown, preventing the
fish from eating normally.

Additional diet items include bloodworms, brine shrimp, daphnia, and finely chopped seafood (shrimp, squid). Dry foods
are typically ignored by puffers. Feed small portions 1–2 times daily, ensuring that snails appear in the diet at least
2–3 times per week. Maintaining a breeding colony of ramshorn snails in a separate tank is a convenient way to ensure a
constant supply of this essential food.

## Care and Maintenance

_Tetraodon biocellatus_ is sensitive to water quality, making regular tank maintenance essential. Weekly water changes
of 25–30 % are mandatory. With each water change, you must restore the marine salt concentration in the fresh water.
Monitor ammonia and nitrite levels — they should be at zero — and keep nitrates below 20 mg/l.

Puffers are prone to ich (white spot disease) and other illnesses when stressed or kept in poor conditions. Many
standard aquarium medications are poorly tolerated by scaleless fish, so treatment should be administered cautiously, at
half doses. Watch the condition of the dental plates: if they become overgrown and interfere with feeding, manual
trimming may be necessary — a procedure best entrusted to an experienced aquarist or veterinarian.

## Compatibility

_Tetraodon biocellatus_ is a territorial and aggressive fish, so selecting tankmates requires careful consideration. The
best option is a species-only tank. When keeping a group of Figure Eight Puffers together, a spacious aquarium with
numerous hiding spots is essential to minimize conflicts.

Slow-moving, long-finned fish (guppies, angelfish, bettas) are absolutely unsuitable as tankmates — the puffer will nip
their fins relentlessly. Small fish that could become prey should also be excluded. Possible companions include
fast-moving fish that tolerate brackish water, such as [Monodactylus](/en/monodactylus-argenteus),
[Scatophagus](/en/scatophagus-argus), or [Brachygobius](/en/brachygobius-doriae). However, success in community keeping
is never guaranteed and depends on the individual temperament of the specific fish.

## Breeding

Breeding _Tetraodon biocellatus_ in aquarium conditions is extremely difficult and barely documented. In the wild,
spawning occurs in secluded spots among mangrove roots, with the male guarding the clutch until the fry hatch. Sexual
dimorphism in this species is minimal, which further complicates pair formation.

To stimulate spawning, conditions closely mimicking nature are required: a spacious tank, numerous hiding places, and
gradual changes in salinity and temperature to simulate seasonal shifts. Even when all conditions are met, successful
captive breeding remains exceptionally rare. The vast majority of Figure Eight Puffers available in the trade are
wild-caught specimens.
