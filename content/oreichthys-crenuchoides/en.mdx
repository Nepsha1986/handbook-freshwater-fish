## Overview

_Oreichthys crenuchoides_ is a small freshwater barb from the Cyprinidae family, native to the waterways of northeastern
India, specifically the states of Assam and West Bengal. This species was scientifically described relatively recently,
in 2008, and has since rapidly gained popularity among aquarists worldwide.

The defining feature of _Oreichthys crenuchoides_ is the spectacular sail-like dorsal fin of the males. It is notably
elongated and resembles a flowing drape, which gave the fish its common name — the Drape Fin Barb. Males raise and
display this magnificent fin during courtship and territorial disputes, making their behavior exceptionally captivating
to observe. Females are more modestly colored and have a standard-shaped dorsal fin.

Adult specimens reach only 4–5 cm in length, making _Oreichthys crenuchoides_ an excellent candidate for nano aquariums.
Their lifespan in captivity is 2–3 years. Despite being related to other barbs, this species is remarkably peaceful and
shows no aggression toward tank mates.

## Tank Requirements

A group of _Oreichthys crenuchoides_ requires an aquarium with a minimum volume of 60 liters. Optimal water parameters
are: temperature 22–26°C, pH 6.0–7.5, and general hardness (GH) 5–12. These fish prefer soft, slightly acidic water that
closely resembles the conditions of their natural biotopes — slow-moving streams and rivers of northeastern India.

The aquarium should be densely planted with live vegetation to provide shelter and create a comfortable environment.
Fine-leaved species such as Java moss, cryptocorynes, and various ferns are particularly suitable. Floating plants will
help create the subdued lighting these fish prefer. The substrate should be fine and dark — sand or fine gravel works
best. Moderate water flow is appreciated, as these fish inhabit flowing waterways in the wild.

## Feeding and Diet

In the wild, _Oreichthys crenuchoides_ feed on small invertebrates, insect larvae, and plant-based microorganisms. In
the aquarium, these fish are undemanding eaters and readily accept a variety of foods.

The staple diet can consist of high-quality micro pellets and flakes appropriately sized for small fish. However, to
maintain health, vibrant coloration, and to encourage the males' display behavior, it is important to regularly offer
live and frozen foods: daphnia, brine shrimp, small bloodworms, and cyclops. Plant-based supplements such as spirulina
or blanched vegetables are also beneficial for a balanced diet. Feed small portions once or twice daily.

## Care and Maintenance

_Oreichthys crenuchoides_ is a relatively undemanding species suitable even for beginners with some experience. The care
level is low, though maintaining stable water parameters is essential.

Weekly water changes of 20–30% are recommended to maintain water quality. Good filtration is necessary, but the current
should not be too strong. These fish should be kept in a group of at least 6–8 individuals — they feel more confident in
a school and display more vivid natural behavior. When kept in groups, males regularly put on impressive displays of
their sail-like dorsal fins, competing for the attention of females.

These fish are active during the daytime. They primarily occupy the middle layers of the water column but will explore
the entire aquarium.

## Compatibility

Thanks to their exceptionally peaceful nature, _Oreichthys crenuchoides_ are perfectly suited for community aquariums
with other small and calm species. Excellent tank mates include [harlequin rasboras](/en/trigonostigma-heteromorpha),
[chili rasboras](/en/boraras-brigittae), [celestial pearl danios](/en/danio-margaritatus),
[pygmy corydoras](/en/corydoras-pygmaeus), and [neon tetras](/en/paracheirodon-innesi). Other peaceful barbs such as the
[cherry barb](/en/puntius-titteya) also make great companions. The related species
[Oreichthys cosuatis](/en/oreichthys-cosuatis) can be kept together as well.

Avoid housing them with large, aggressive, or overly active species that may intimidate these small fish or damage their
delicate elongated fins. Large cichlids and boisterous barbs like the [tiger barb](/en/puntius-tetrazona) are not
suitable tank mates.

## Breeding

Breeding _Oreichthys crenuchoides_ in a home aquarium is achievable, though it requires some preparation. The breeding
difficulty is considered moderate.

A separate breeding tank with soft, slightly acidic water (pH around 6.5, GH no more than 8) and a temperature of
24–26°C is recommended. Fine-leaved plants or a spawning grid should be placed on the bottom to protect the eggs from
being eaten by the parents. Subdued lighting helps stimulate spawning behavior.

Before spawning, males actively display their magnificent dorsal fins, spreading them wide before the females in an
impressive courtship dance. These fish are egg scatterers: the female deposits adhesive eggs among the plants. After
spawning, the adults must be removed to prevent egg predation. Incubation lasts 24–36 hours, and after a few days the
fry become free-swimming. Suitable first foods include infusoria and brine shrimp nauplii.
