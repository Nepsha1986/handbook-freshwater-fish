## Overview

_Pangio semicincta_ is a small freshwater loach belonging to the family Cobitidae, native to waterways of Southeast Asia
— specifically the Malay Peninsula, Sumatra, and Borneo. This fish reaches a size of 6–8 cm and has a characteristic
elongated, eel-like body. Its base coloration is a warm yellow-orange adorned with dark brown or black transverse bands.
The key distinguishing feature of _Pangio semicincta_ from the closely related [Kuhli Loach](/en/pangio-kuhlii) is that
the dark bands do not encircle the body completely but only cover the upper half — this is precisely why the species is
called "half-banded" (semicincta meaning "half-girdled"). These two species are frequently confused in the aquarium
trade, so it is worth carefully examining the banding pattern when purchasing.

_Pangio semicincta_ is a nocturnal species that prefers to hide among plants, driftwood, and within soft substrate
during the day. As dusk falls, the fish become active and begin scouring the bottom in search of food. This is a social
species that should be kept in groups of at least 5 — in the company of conspecifics, the loaches feel more confident
and are more likely to be visible. With proper care, their lifespan in an aquarium ranges from 5 to 10 years.

## Tank Requirements

A group of _Pangio semicincta_ requires an aquarium with a minimum volume of 60 liters. Optimal water parameters are:
temperature 24–28 °C, pH 6.0–7.5, and general hardness (GH) 5–12. A soft sandy substrate is essential — these loaches
actively burrow into the bottom, and sharp gravel particles can injure their delicate body and sensitive barbels.

The aquarium should be densely planted and equipped with plenty of hiding spots made from driftwood, rocks, and ceramic
tubes. Subdued lighting will help replicate conditions similar to their natural shaded streams and reduce stress levels.
It is critically important to have a tightly fitting lid — _Pangio semicincta_ are notorious escape artists capable of
squeezing through the smallest gaps. Filter intakes should also be covered with fine mesh or sponge to prevent the small
loaches from being drawn inside.

## Feeding and Diet

In the wild, _Pangio semicincta_ feeds on small invertebrates, insect larvae, and organic detritus found in bottom
sediments. In the aquarium, the staple diet should consist of high-quality sinking pellets and wafers designed for
bottom-dwelling fish. The diet should be supplemented with live or frozen foods such as bloodworms, brine shrimp,
daphnia, and tubifex worms.

Since this is a nocturnal species, feeding is best done in the evening, shortly before lights out, or even in complete
darkness. This ensures the food reaches the loaches rather than being consumed by more active daytime inhabitants. It is
important that the food sinks to the bottom — this is where _Pangio semicincta_ searches for it, meticulously combing
through the substrate with their sensitive barbels.

## Care and Maintenance

_Pangio semicincta_ is a relatively undemanding species with a care level of 2 out of 5, but several important
guidelines must be followed to keep the fish healthy. Weekly water changes of 20–25% will help maintain stable water
quality. Test water parameters regularly, paying special attention to ammonia and nitrite levels, to which loaches are
particularly sensitive.

The sandy substrate requires occasional light vacuuming, but this should be done carefully to avoid disturbing burrowed
fish. Always count your loaches during tank maintenance — their secretive lifestyle makes it easy to overlook a missing
individual. Pay attention to skin condition: like other scaleless fish, _Pangio semicincta_ are sensitive to medications
containing copper and formalin, so half-doses should be used when treating diseases in the community tank.

## Compatibility

_Pangio semicincta_ is a peaceful species with an aggression level of 2 out of 5 that is well suited for community
aquariums. Ideal tank mates are small, peaceful fish inhabiting the middle and upper water layers:
[neon tetras](/en/paracheirodon-innesi), [harlequin rasboras](/en/trigonostigma-heteromorpha), and
[ember tetras](/en/hyphessobrycon-amandae). They also coexist well with other peaceful bottom dwellers such as
[corydoras](/en/corydoras-panda) and other Pangio species, including the [Kuhli Loach](/en/pangio-kuhlii).

_Pangio semicincta_ must be kept in groups of no fewer than 5 individuals. When kept alone or in pairs, the loaches
become extremely shy and rarely emerge from hiding. In a group, however, they display fascinating social behavior, often
piling together in a single hiding spot. Avoid housing them with large or aggressive species such as large cichlids,
which may view the small loaches as prey.

## Breeding

Breeding _Pangio semicincta_ in captivity is a challenging endeavor (difficulty level 4 out of 5), and successful
spawning in home aquariums is extremely rare. These loaches are egg scatterers and do not exhibit any parental care.

To stimulate spawning, it is recommended to raise the water temperature to the upper end of the acceptable range (26–28
°C), soften the water, and provide an abundance of fine-leaved plants or spawning mops where the female can deposit her
eggs. Spawning is sometimes triggered by simulating the rainy season — performing a large water change with soft, cool
water. After spawning, the adults must be removed, as they will eat the eggs. The fry hatch within a few days and
initially feed on infusoria and microworms before transitioning to newly hatched brine shrimp.
