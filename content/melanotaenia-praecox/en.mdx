## Overview

_Melanotaenia praecox_, known as the Dwarf Neon Rainbowfish or Praecox Rainbowfish, is one of the smallest members of
the Melanotaeniidae family. Adults reach only 5-6 cm in length, making them an excellent choice for medium-sized
aquariums. In the wild, this species inhabits the Mamberamo River system in northern Papua (Indonesia), where it lives
in clear streams and tributaries with moderate current.

The most distinctive feature of the Dwarf Neon Rainbowfish is its bright neon-blue body that shimmers as the light angle
changes. Sexual dimorphism is well pronounced: males have red fin edges, while females display orange to yellow fin
edges. Males are also larger and more intensely colored than females. These active fish require ample swimming space and
look best in a school of six or more individuals.

## Tank Requirements

Dwarf Neon Rainbowfish require an aquarium of at least 80 liters. Despite their small size, they are active swimmers
that need plenty of open space for free movement. Recommended water parameters are: temperature 24-28°C, pH 6.5-7.5, and
general hardness (GH) 8-12 dGH.

The aquarium should be densely planted along the sides and back, leaving the central area open for swimming. Good
filtration with moderate flow replicates their natural habitat conditions. A tight-fitting lid is essential, as
rainbowfish are prone to jumping. Weekly water changes of 20-30% will help maintain stable water quality.

## Feeding and Diet

_Melanotaenia praecox_ is an omnivorous species that feeds on small insects, larvae, crustaceans, and plant matter in
the wild. In the aquarium, the staple diet should consist of high-quality flake or small-granule foods.

To maintain vibrant coloration and good health, the diet should be regularly supplemented with live or frozen foods such
as daphnia, brine shrimp, bloodworms, and cyclops. Vegetable supplements like spirulina flakes or blanched spinach help
provide balanced nutrition. Feed two to three times daily in small portions that the fish consume within a few minutes.

## Care and Maintenance

The care level for Dwarf Neon Rainbowfish is relatively low, making them suitable for aquarists with some basic
experience. The key requirement is maintaining stable water parameters. These fish are sensitive to sudden fluctuations
in temperature and pH, so regular water changes and parameter monitoring are particularly important.

Weekly water changes of 20-30%, substrate vacuuming, and nitrate level monitoring form the foundation of stable
maintenance. With proper care, their lifespan is 3-5 years. It is important to ensure good aeration and avoid
overstocking the aquarium.

## Compatibility

The Dwarf Neon Rainbowfish is a peaceful schooling fish that makes an excellent addition to a community aquarium. They
should be kept in groups of at least six to maintain natural schooling behavior and bring out the best coloration in
males.

Good tank mates include other rainbowfish such as the [Boesemani Rainbowfish](/en/melanotaenia-boesemani), as well as
peaceful species of similar size: [Zebra Danios](/en/danio-rerio), [Scissortail Rasboras](/en/rasbora-trilineata), and
bottom dwellers like [Panda Corydoras](/en/corydoras-panda) or [Sterbai Corydoras](/en/corydoras-sterbai). Avoid large
and aggressive fish that may bully or prey on the small rainbowfish.

## Breeding

Breeding _Melanotaenia praecox_ in the home aquarium is quite achievable. For spawning, prepare a separate tank with a
temperature of 26-28°C and fine-leaved plants or dedicated spawning mops. Fish reach sexual maturity at around six
months of age.

Males display especially vivid coloration during the breeding period and actively court females. Spawning typically
occurs in the morning hours. The female deposits several adhesive eggs daily, attaching them to plants or substrate.
After spawning, it is recommended to remove the adults, as they may consume their own eggs.

The eggs develop over 7-10 days depending on temperature. Newly hatched fry should be fed infusoria or liquid fry food,
gradually transitioning to baby brine shrimp as they grow.
